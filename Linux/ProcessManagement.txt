üßµ 3. Background and Foreground Jobs

    Linux shells allow multitasking ‚Äî you can run processes in the foreground or background.

    ‚ñ∂Ô∏è Foreground Job

        When you run a command normally:
            ping google.com
        It runs in the foreground and blocks the terminal until you stop it (Ctrl+C).

    ‚è∏Ô∏è Background Job

        To run a process in the background, add an ampersand (&):
            ping google.com &
        Now, the shell immediately returns control to you.

    üìã Managing Jobs

        Key Commands:

            Command      Description
            jobs         Lists current jobs with their IDs.
            fg %1        Brings job 1 to the foreground.
            bg %1        Resumes a stopped job in the background.
            kill %1      Kills job number 1.

        Example session:

            sleep 100 &
            jobs           # [1] 1234
            fg %1          # Brings sleep job to foreground
            Ctrl+Z         # Suspends it
            bg %1          # Runs it again in background

    üõ†Ô∏è Job States

        Running    ‚Äì process executing normally
        Stopped    ‚Äì suspended with Ctrl+Z
        Terminated ‚Äì process finished or killed

üß± Detaching and Disowning Jobs

    If you close the terminal, background jobs will stop unless detached.

    To prevent that:

        disown

    nohup (‚Äúno hangup‚Äù) makes process ignore the hangup signal.
    disown removes it from the shell‚Äôs job table (it won‚Äôt die if you log out).
